Data-Driven Decision Making (DDDM)

This project uses data-driven decision making by analyzing booking, pricing, and customer behavior data to support business decisions.

The analysis was performed using Python in Anaconda Jupyter Notebook, and the final script was converted into a .py file for structured submission.

Key decisions based on data:

1. Helsinki shows highest revenue performance, so expansion of premium storage capacity is recommended.

2. Revenue trends show increased demand during summer months, so marketing campaigns should be increased before peak season.

3. Turku shows lower performance compared to other cities, suggesting pricing and promotional strategy should be reviewed.

4. Correlation analysis shows a positive relationship between bookings and revenue, confirming that increasing bookings will directly improve revenue.

5. Repeat customer rate is around 50%, indicating strong customer retention and opportunity to expand loyalty programs.

These insights demonstrate how data is used to guide strategic and operational decisions.
